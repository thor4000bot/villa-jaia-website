The Philosophy

Unhurried. Considered. Entirely Yours.

Villa Magnus was conceived as an antidote to the ordinary. Surrounded by hundreds of ancient olive trees on the limestone plateau between Noto and the sea, it is a place where time slows, beauty is everywhere, and silence becomes a luxury in itself.

The Pool

A Horizon Without End

The centrepiece of Villa Magnus is a 12-metre chlorine-free infinity pool whose edge dissolves into the Ionian Sea beyond. Saline, sun-warmed, and utterly silent — it is the defining memory every guest takes home.

Sunbeds, shade, and the scent of olive trees. Nothing more is needed.

The Interiors

Natural Materials, Considered Detail

Three spacious bedrooms dressed in natural rattan, linen, and cotton. Light-filled, serene, and designed to feel like a considered home rather than a hotel room.

A fully equipped kitchen, expansive living area, and a terrace that was made for long evenings and unhurried dinners.

The Land

Ancient Olive Trees & Open Sky

The villa sits within a vast private garden of hundreds of ancient olive trees — a working orchard that has shaped this landscape for centuries. It provides complete privacy, deep shade, and a connection to place that no resort can replicate.

The Region

Sicily at Your Doorstep

Villa Magnus sits at the heart of the most beautiful corner of Sicily. Everything worth seeing is within an hour — here are our personal recommendations.

01

Marzamemi

A perfectly preserved fishing village with one of Sicily's most beautiful piazzas. Start with a focaccia at Premiata Focacceria Mazzini, browse the tuna conserves at Campisi, and end with an aperitivo on the waterfront. A full half-day.

20 min
02

Noto

The crown jewel of Sicilian Baroque — a UNESCO World Heritage city built entirely from honey-gold limestone. Wander the Corso, visit the cathedral at sunset, and stop at Caffè Sicilia for what many consider the best granita in Italy.

15 min
03

Calamosche Beach

Consistently voted one of Italy's most beautiful beaches — a wild, protected cove within the Vendicari Nature Reserve. No sun loungers, no crowds, no noise. Just white sand and crystal water. Arrive early in summer.

30 min
04

Planeta Winery

One of Sicily's most celebrated estates, and the closest to the villa. Call ahead to book a cellar tour and tasting — the Nero d'Avola and Fiano are exceptional. The setting among ancient vines is worth the visit alone.

20 min
05

Modica

The chocolate capital of Sicily — a steep, dramatic Baroque city where the ancient Aztec recipe for cold-process chocolate is still made. Visit Antica Dolceria Bonajuto (founded 1880) and don't leave without a bar of chilli chocolate.

30 min
06

Ortigia · Siracusa

An island city that was once the most powerful Greek colony in the Mediterranean. The Greek temple embedded in the cathedral facade, the Fonte Aretusa spring, and the evening passeggiata along the waterfront are not to be missed.

45 min
